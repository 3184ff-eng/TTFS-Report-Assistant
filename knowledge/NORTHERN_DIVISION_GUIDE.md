# Northern Division Guide To Completion Of Fire Reports

## Core Standard

Fire reports must be clear, factual, chronological where appropriate, and complete enough for Fire Prevention review. The officer must not guess or invent missing information. If a fact is not known, it must be flagged as missing, not supplied by the AI.

## Officer's Observations

Officer's Observations must contain only what the officer observed from arrival at the incident scene until departure. This includes visible fire, smoke, damage, hazards, firefighting actions observed or performed by the crew, appliances present, and the condition of the scene during Fire Service attendance.

Do not place witness statements, caller information, occupier statements, police information, or actions before Fire Service arrival in Officer's Observations.

## Additional Information

Additional Information is the correct place for witness statements, information received, investigation findings, caller reports, occupier statements, and actions that occurred before Fire Service arrival. When wording uses phrases such as "witness stated", "the owner informed", "it was reported", "before arrival", or "prior to arrival", it must be separated from Officer's Observations.

## Property Description

The report should identify the property type, use, construction class or material where known, number of storeys, approximate size where known, occupancy, affected room or area, and exposures. If construction, size, use, or occupancy is not known, flag it.

## Extinguishment Narrative

The report should state how the fire was extinguished, including the method, extinguishing agent, hose line or equipment used, water source if known, appliances involved, and whether the fire was extinguished before or after Fire Service arrival. Do not invent appliances, water source, or tactics.

## Damage Description

Damage should be described clearly and separately where possible: fire damage, heat damage, smoke damage, water damage, structural damage, contents/stock damage, and any exposure damage. Values and insurance details must be recorded in their separate official form fields.

## Cause Determination

Use only the official cause classifications: Natural, Accidental, Incendiary, Undetermined. Do not convert probable ignition-source wording into an official cause classification unless the officer has stated that classification. If cause support is incomplete, flag the missing information.

## Personnel And Casualties

Personnel details should preserve service numbers, ranks, and names exactly. Casualties must be entered into their table categories: Name, Brief description of injuries, and Treated by. Do not combine casualty information into a narrative when the official table fields are available.
