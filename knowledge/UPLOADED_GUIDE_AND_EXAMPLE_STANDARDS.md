# Uploaded Guide and Example Standards

This file records project guidance extracted for retrieval from the uploaded/available TTFS guide and example reports:

- `knowledge/Guide to FIRE REPORTS.pdf`
- `examples/TRINIDAD AND TOBAGO FIRE  REPORT- Light Pole Fire(1).pdf`
- `examples/TRINIDAD AND TOBAGO FIRE  REPORT-Regency Star Hotel.pdf`

The AI must use these notes as report-writing and field-placement standards only. Do not treat example facts as facts for a new incident.

## Field Placement Rules

- Station must be one of the official TTFS station dropdown options grouped by headquarters.
- Incident type belongs in the official incident/type field and must not be merged into station.
- Officer's Observations must be restricted to what the officer observed from arrival to departure.
- Information from occupiers, neighbours, callers, police, utility providers, investigation units, or other third parties belongs in Additional Information.
- Actions before Fire Service arrival belong in Additional Information.
- Investigation narrative, including area of origin, ignition source, first material ignited, fire patterns, and probable cause reasoning, belongs in Additional Information unless a supported cause classification is explicitly selected.
- Property construction, occupancy, dimensions, class of construction, number of storeys, use, and exposures belong in Type of Property.
- Extinguishment method, hose lines, jets, water/foam/dry powder use, isolation, overhaul, and hotspot checks belong in How Fire Was Extinguished.
- Burn damage, smoke damage, heat damage, water damage, damaged contents, damaged stock, damaged vehicle components, damaged poles, and damage estimates belong in Description of Damage or the value/damage fields.
- Values must be split into Value of Building, Value of Stock, Damage to Building, Damage to Stock, and Insurance Details where figures are available.
- Casualty details must be split into the casualty table: name, injury, and treated by.

## Professional Report Style

- Use clear chronological professional language.
- Preserve service numbers, ranks, names, addresses, appliance numbers, times, vehicle registrations, money values, and official cause classifications exactly.
- Correct grammar, spelling, punctuation, capitalization, and sentence structure without changing facts.
- Do not invent missing details. Flag missing or unclear information.
- Avoid unsupported cause conclusions. Use only Natural, Accidental, Incendiary, or Undetermined when the officer explicitly confirms the classification.

## Light Pole Fire Standards

- Capture pole number or nearest landmark if provided.
- Record visible smoke, flame, arcing, sparks, electrical hazard, and exposures observed after arrival.
- Record whether T&TEC or another utility provider was contacted or on scene under Additional Information unless directly observed during the Fire Service attendance.
- Extinguishment should describe safe isolation, extinguishing agent used, standby, cooling, and scene safety checks where provided.
- Damage should identify the pole, wires, transformer/fuse/cutout, surrounding vegetation, property, or other exposures affected.

## Commercial or Large-Structure Standards

- Record business/occupancy, construction type, dimensions, number of storeys, affected area, stock/contents, and exposures.
- Keep investigation findings, area of origin, first material ignited, ignition source, and fire spread explanation in Additional Information unless directly observed by the reporting officer during attendance.
- Separate building value, stock/content value, building damage, stock/content damage, and insurance information.
- Damage descriptions should identify affected rooms/areas, smoke/heat/water damage, stock damage, and structural damage without overstating facts.
