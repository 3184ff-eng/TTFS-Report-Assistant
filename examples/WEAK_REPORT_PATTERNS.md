# Weak Report Patterns

Placeholder for examples of report issues to detect and improve.

## Patterns To Capture

- Invented or assumed facts
- Witness statements placed in Officer's Observations
- Pre-arrival actions placed in Officer's Observations
- Unsupported cause classification
- Missing required information
- Vague chronology

## Weak Observation Pattern

Weak: "On arrival, the owner said the fire started in the bedroom before the brigade came."

Problem: The owner statement and pre-arrival information do not belong in Officer's Observations. Move it to Additional Information and flag cause support if the officer did not observe the origin or cause.

## Weak Damage Pattern

Weak: "The building was damaged."

Problem: This does not identify fire, smoke, heat, water, structural, stock, or contents damage. Ask for or flag the affected area and type of damage.

## Weak Extinguishment Pattern

Weak: "The fire was out."

Problem: This does not state how the fire was extinguished, what equipment or agent was used, or whether the fire was extinguished before or after Fire Service arrival.

## Weak Cause Pattern

Weak: "The cause was electrical" or "probably electrical."

Problem: Electrical is not one of the permitted official cause classifications. Use only Natural, Accidental, Incendiary, or Undetermined when the officer states a supported classification.

## Weak Casualty Pattern

Weak: "Some persons were injured."

Problem: Casualty details must be placed in the casualty table categories where known: Name, Brief description of injuries, and Treated by. Missing names, injury descriptions, or treatment details must be flagged.
