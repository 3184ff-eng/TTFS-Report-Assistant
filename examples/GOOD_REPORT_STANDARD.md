# Good Report Standard

Placeholder for examples and traits of strong TTFS fire reports.

## Expected Qualities

- Fact-based
- Complete
- Chronological where appropriate
- Clear separation between observed facts and received information
- Uses only approved cause classifications
- Flags unknown or missing information

## Good Officer's Observations Pattern

On arrival at the scene, the crew observed smoke issuing from the kitchen area of the dwelling house. Fire damage was observed to the kitchen cupboards and adjacent wall area. Smoke staining was observed to the ceiling and upper wall surfaces. The fire was extinguished using the stated equipment and agent. The scene was made safe before the crew departed.

This pattern is good because it only states what was observed or done during Fire Service attendance. It does not include what the owner, witness, caller, or neighbour said.

## Good Additional Information Pattern

The owner informed the officer that smoke was seen before Fire Service arrival. This information is recorded as received information and was not treated as the officer's direct observation. Insurance information was not ascertained.

This pattern is good because it separates received information from Officer's Observations and flags missing insurance information.

## Good Damage And Values Pattern

Description of Damage: Fire damage was confined to the kitchen cupboards and adjacent wall area. Smoke damage was observed to the kitchen ceiling and upper wall surfaces.

Value of Building: 500,000
Value of Stock: Not applicable
Damage to Building: 25,000
Damage to Stock: Not applicable
Building and Stock Insured as follows: Insurance information was not ascertained.

This pattern is good because values and insurance details are kept in their separate official fields.

## Good Casualty Pattern

Casualty table entries should preserve table categories:

- Name: John Doe
- Brief description of injuries: Smoke inhalation
- Treated by: Ambulance personnel

If there are no casualties, leave casualty rows blank or state no casualties in the appropriate report narrative; do not invent names or treatment.
